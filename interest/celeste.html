Modded Celeste

Celeste is a precision platforming video game released in 2018 by Maddie Makes Games. Modded celeste refers to the 3rd party modifications (mods) and maps that the celeste community has made for the game.

FAQ

Question Answer
How do I install mods? The most common way to install mods is the Everest mod loader.
Where can I find mods? Mods can be found on GameBanana, the Celeste Discord, or through the Everest launcher.
Can I play mods on console? No, mods are only available for the PC version.
Do mods cost money? No, most mods are free to download and play.
How do I make mods? For making custom maps, the map making tool Lonn is the most well supported by the community.
Where do I start? After installing Everest, collabs are a great way to start given their wide range of map difficulties and generally high quality. Strawberry Jam Collab and Spring Collab are two of the most popular.
What do the difficulties mean? The difficulties used by the modding community are: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, Expert, and GM. Each tier also has subtiers of Green, Yellow and Red.
What are goldens? Golden strawberries, or just goldens, are a collectable rewarded for completing a map deathless. To "Golden" a map means to beat it start to finish without dying.
What is "tech"? Movement tech refers to any advanced form of movement beyond the basic jump, dash and grab in the base game. Some examples include super dashes, hyper dashes, wave dashes, wall bounces, and ultra dashes.
Why do people mod celeste so much? Celeste has a number of features that make it ideal for high-level platforming gameplay. It has a fixed framerate and pixel grid, allowing for very precise movement. It also has a very expressive move set and deep physics system, leading to a high skill ceiling.
